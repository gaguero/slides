Nayara Bocas del Toro - Drip Campaign Redesign

Left: Findings and proposal. Right: New 6 email sequence in agent voice. Use the controls below each deck to move between slides.

Slide 1 of 4

Context and Problem

Findings

Current setup

  • Two enrollment paths: Two forms on our website—Get Exclusive Offers (GEO form) and You Need Assistance (YNA form)—feed the same automated email sequence.
  • Automation + human follow-up: Both forms automatically enroll leads into the legacy drip campaign while agents simultaneously launch their manual outreach.
  • High-touch contact: Agents email, text, and call within minutes of every form submission, as expected by leadership.

Resulting issues

  • Leads get overlapping Email 1 messages (automated + manual) delivered almost simultaneously.
  • Voice is not consistent (team inbox vs individual agent identity), creating mixed expectations.
  • The experience feels messy for a luxury guest even though the follow-up intensity is high.

Key Insights

Findings
  • The manual first email used by agents—refined by Kelly over time—is proven to drive quick replies.
  • Dan expects very proactive outreach: immediate email, SMS and phone contact when a lead arrives.
  • Guests who fill the YNA form often share dates and specific needs, and expect individualized help rather than a generic brochure style message.
  • The main problem is not level of contact but lack of orchestration and consistent voice.

Proposed Flow (GEO vs YNA)

Proposal

Get Exclusive Offers (GEO form)

  • Keep automatic enrollment in the drip campaign, but replace the old Email 1 with the new, tested first email created by Kelly.
  • Email 1 introduces the assigned agent, presents the current key promotion and rate table, and provides clear next steps.
  • Emails 2 to 6 follow automatically as follow-up, all sent from the same assigned agent's email address.
  • The promotional offers section only needs periodic updates, coordinated by the sales team with our HubSpot vendor.

You Need Assistance (YNA form)

  • Do not send Email 1 automatically.
  • The agent reads the form details and sends a tailored version of Email 1 manually, using the same proven structure but adapted to the guest's specific dates and questions.
  • After sending the personalized Email 1, the agent enrolls the contact into the automated drip campaign starting at Email 2.

Justification and Next Steps

Proposal

Why this works

  • One primary first email concept for both forms, aligned with what agents already know performs well.
  • All emails now speak in the same agent centered voice: I, me, my, and clear contact pathways.
  • Keeps aggressive follow up but removes confusion for the guest.

Implementation notes

  • In HubSpot, map GEO to the automated 6 email sequence starting at new Email 1.
  • For YNA, provide agents with a ready template for Email 1 and a simple way to start the sequence at Email 2.
  • Ensure workflows stop sending as soon as a lead responds through any channel, or if status is Booked / opted out.
